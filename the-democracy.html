The Democracy

  •   Mon 16 April 2018
  •   Thoughts
  •   politics india democracy philosophy constituion

It is said that the best democracies are not the ones where everything seem to be going perfect. Because, in those there may lie a silent group without their voices being heard. Struggling to even make a feable sound heard over the defening noise of concensus.

The best ones are who can take a test and still come out as democracies. In liberty, fraternity, brotherhood and justice. To maintain and strive for equality and respect in front of the law.

It is only natural that societies move towards extremes given due time. Give enough time and we shall instill such liberal ideas that we fall into the rule of corporations or the economically strong. Give enough time and we shall find ourselves in hand of a tough and over reaching government.

The greatest catchbin this however is the tipping point. The event or scenario where the majority of the population decides enough is enough. This could be as simple as few men having beer decide to change things for better or a nation coming together over few years to rewrite their constitution. However, there is this element that decodes what would be the path this change takes. One can sum that up in the following quote.

Those who make peaceful revolution impossible will make violent revolution inevitable

-JFK

At these critical tipping points if there exist good mechanisms for the citizens to address the issues within the system there will be a smooth transition from one state to another. If on the other hand the situation is where the current entities have taken away all means of civil appeal for change what we shall is a chaotic and turbulant transition that may cost us a lot.

It is only right and desirable that the democracies in countries come under test in regular intervals. The caution is always only regarding whether such tests would go down as a mark of loss due to the lack of proper machienary and the people will have to turn to the crudent methods known to man to get the transition done.

It is not in our brightest times that we learn the value of the words put down in the holy book that is the constitution but in the darkest times where men and women see the glowing golden thread of the mind's past that seemed justice, liberty and equality to their brothers.

One can only hope the storm that has been brewing will find an alley to blow through, taking the litter and garbage along with it than tearing down the buildings so new ones need to be built from the scrats.

In people we trust. People who hope for a better tomorrow.

Cheers
